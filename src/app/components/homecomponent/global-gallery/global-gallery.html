Life-Giving Water, Life-Giving Women

Women in Malaysia’s Kedah River Basin play vital roles in sustaining communities as the river supports fishing, agriculture, and livelihoods. Artisans like Zanariyan Binti Azizan show how local economies are shaped by the river. Centering women in water governance leads to more equitable, resilient systems essential for food security and climate adaptation.

South Sudan: Grateful for water, but is help coming?

In Malakal, South Sudan, 45,000 displaced people rely on a consistent clean water supply drawn from the Nile, even as funding cuts erode health care. Residents fear leaving the site due to violence. "Communities urgently question whether broader humanitarian aid will arrive to meet their essential needs."
